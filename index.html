Embedded microprocessors

Microprocessors are tiny, bare basic computers. With no operating system, input devices, or output devices by standard, it's essentially a blank scrapbook to the digital world. From such a small device, however, you can do powerful things. Because of their small and open nature, MCU's, for short, can do things you might not've imagined. They can be sent into all sorts of situations, gather data from practically every sensor known to mankind, process things on board, or of course outsource the data to a computer, television, phone, or the internet.
